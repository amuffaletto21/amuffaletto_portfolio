Art:

I enjoy making pretty much any type of art, however my favorite will always be drawing. I learned how to draw three years ago at my arts focused middle school, and loved it since then. While I was there I learned how to draw with different medias (oil pastels, charcoal, colored pencils, etc) and many different genres as well such as surrealism, still life, and abstraction. Currently, my favorite style of drawing is realism/still life, and my favorite medias are markers and pencil. While I was studying art at my school, I entered a few art competitions both within the school and outside the school. The awards that I have won at my school are a banner contest and an art show contest. The banner contest was school wide for the art students to have their banner hung in the interior or exterior of the school. The materials I used for this were markers and colored pencils. There were a total of 15 spots available to win, and I won one of the places on the inside of the school. The art show contest was also school wide for all the art students and there were three spots to win this time in order to have your art showcased and featured during the spring art show. I had won first place with a piece in the style of Paul Klee. For this, I used watercolor for the background and used multi-colored tracing paper to put the drawing on the watercolor background. In addition, I've had two drawings of mine hung up in the school outside of a competition. The first piece was a heavy contrast piece in an abstraction style, and the second one was an altered self-portrait. Both were made with markers. Outside of the school, I won 3rd place in a Scholastic contest with the theme of "Gold." For this contest I drew an adaptation of King Midas turning a water fountain into gold. This work was made with colored pencils. In addition to drawing, I also enjoy doing thread work and other woven arts like embroidery, weaving, and beading (making them out of wool and clay).

Rowing:

Before coming to Andover, I had no idea what crew was or how to row, but I thought I would try it out in fall term and I loved it. I started rowing on an 8-boat in the stern pair (which just means the first two seats from the front of the boat). Since then, I've rowed in fours (four person boat) with mens oars, and in the Tail of the Merrimack with the rest of the people in Fall Crew. I wasn't able to compete in the Spring season because of a wrist injury I got from crew, but this year I hope to be able to do both Fall and Spring crew.

Calligraphy:

I decided to start calligraphy because I saw some intersting instagram videos by people like Seb Lester and Aaria Baid. From watching these videos, I became intrigued by calligraphy and then looked up online tutorials for faux calligraphy (calligraphy done without a brush pen or a quill). After I became comfortable enough with faux calligraphy, I bought my first quill set with two pots of ink and brush pens, to do real calligraphy.

Accordion:

I learned how to play the accordion in first grade, and continued playing it for 3 years. I started out with a smaller accordion, and then as I grew older and better at the accordion, the instrument itself got a lot bigger as well. Once I reached the point where I had to buy a heavier and harder to manage accordion, I ultimately stopped playing the instrument. Even though I quit the instrument, I still think that it is very intriguing, and at some point in the future I've thought about going back to it.
